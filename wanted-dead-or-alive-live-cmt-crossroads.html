Overall Meaning

The lyrics to Bon Jovi’s “Wanted Dead or Alive” speak to the romanticized image of the outlaw, a lone ranger who roams the land with nothing but a guitar and a thirst for adventure. The first verse sets the scene for the song, with the idea that the singer is a wandering soul who is stuck in a world where everything is the same – much like the life of a nomad, where they live in a repetitive cycle. The line ‘Every day, it seems we're wastin' away’ suggests a life of meaningless and purposelessness. The second verse details the loneliness that comes with the outlaw lifestyle, with the people the singer meets always leaving and the bottle being a constant companion. The chorus ‘I'm a cowboy, on a steel horse I ride’ is a metaphor for the singer’s guitar, and this likening himself to a cowboy on a motorcycle is perhaps an extension of the Wild West outlaw persona.


The last verse tells of the singer’s triumphs as a musical outlaw, claiming to have been ‘everywhere’ and ‘seen a million faces and rocked them all’. This suggests that the life of the outlaw isn’t always a sad and lonely one but can be a thrilling adventure. Throughout the song, the chorus repeats with the words ‘Wanted dead or alive’, a phrase that speaks to the idea of the outlaw being a target of the law – where it feels like the only options are to either die or to carry on living their life as a rebel. Ultimately, the lyrics of “Wanted Dead or Alive” relate to the idea of freedom, where the life of an outlaw is one that is independent and free from the constraints of society.
